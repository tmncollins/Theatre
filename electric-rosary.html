It’s commonplace: bombs go off at night and people disappear before dawn. None of that’s newsworthy. Everyday proceedings have their complications; some are fatal, some are more irritating. It’s a messy business - keeping the peace, controlling the masses, making sure everybody and everything is held on a tight leash.

Once, there were wars abroad. Now, there are wars at home. There’s fighting on the streets, and paperwork piling up on desks. These days money is scarce and food is scarcer. Any job is a job, if you keep your eyes and ears closed. And yet here we are: bringing love into a world of hate.

Cohen is a psychiatrist… was a psychiatrist. Now he needs a job. Soon, he will come face-to-face with a revolution, and a little too close with his past.

Toby Collins’ set design helped show a convent which was down on its luck, underfunded and sparsely decorated, filled with an array of random clutter and unused congregation chairs. This was really effective in showing the emptiness and remoteness of the convent and made the threat of violent Luddite protestors feel even more real.

Varsity


When Cohen frees the leader of a revolution, he is arrested and interrogated by his ex-wife.

Cohen, an ex-military psychiatrist, needs a job and applies at the Love Corporation. This company runs a private secret police for an authoritarian regime. He is interviewed by Mr White and Mr Black, two long-time employees who are tired of the bureaucracy to which they are bound. Also working here is Jorg, the leader of a revolutionary movement who is masquerading as a janitor, and Dr Jett, Cohen’s ex-wife and occupational torturer. The play is set in the near-future under a hyper-capitalist and authoritarian government.

This play aims to hold a mirror to both the cruelty and violence we humans can exhibit but also the care and love we can show to strangers. It highlights the dangers of the concentration of power and money in the hands of the few, as well as the results of failing to hold those people to account for their actions.
