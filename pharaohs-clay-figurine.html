They'll come again if she cries any louder, but we don't want them to come. If we sleep, they won't come. They only come when we are loud.

A group of children, trapped in the dark of night, become haunted by the arrival of a mysterious new child, leading them to confront their fears whilst terror threatens to cross the line between imagination and reality.

This production is ambitious with strong moments. It is a gripping and intentional show with interesting themes of humanity and love, and a well-pitched take on the psychological thriller genre with lots of potential.

The Cambridge Student


The Pharaoh's Clay Figurine is a short one-act play that takes you inside the minds of a group of young children, huddled together in a cold, dimly lit room. The arrival of a new child - who is just as scared as they are - causes fear, paranoia, and imagination to take over. What starts as a simple night of whispers turns into a nightmarish exploration of what happens when childhood terrors come to life.

The production is a thought-provoking investigation of fear, innocence, and the power of imagination
