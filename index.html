Frequently Asked Questions

1. When is your next show?

I have a couple of pieces in the works at the moment, including a stage play, radio play, and short film. Check back in Autumn for the big reveal...

2. Can you design the set for my show/event/film?

Unfortunately, I am not considering requests at the moment. However, I will be adding a range of advice to the site soon!

3. Can we stage one of your plays?

Love Corporation and One and a Part are both available to license for amateur and professional productions. Please get in contact if interested.

4. Do you offer merch?

I don't currently have any merch on sale. However, if you would like to purchase a copy of a theatre poster (signed or unsigned) don't hesitate to get in touch!

Varsity

Through its poetry, Collins’ play challenges its audience to think about the complexities of tragedy within family life and womanhood more broadly; raising numerous questions about the value of beauty, trauma, and forgiveness.

Electric Rosary

Varsity

Toby Collins’ set design helped show a convent which was down on its luck, underfunded and sparsely decorated, filled with an array of random clutter and unused congregation chairs. This was really effective in showing the emptiness and remoteness of the convent and made the threat of violent Luddite protestors feel even more real.
